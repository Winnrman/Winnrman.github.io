Our Story

Built out of

frustration.

Index didn't start as a product idea. It started as a very annoying afternoon.

"

A while back, I was digging through old files looking for a project I'd worked on in college. I knew it existed. I remembered bits of it — the problem it solved, a few lines of code, a rough idea of the structure. What I couldn't remember was what I'd named the folder, or the file, or honestly anything useful.

So I searched. And searched. Renamed guesses. Opened folders I hadn't touched in years. Nothing. The file was somewhere on my machine — I just had no way to ask it a question like "do you contain the thing I'm thinking of?"

That's when it clicked: the problem wasn't that the file was lost. The problem was that every search tool I had only knew about names and dates — not what was actually inside. If I couldn't remember what I called it, I had nothing.

"If I can't find the name, maybe I can search for something I remember."

That one thought turned into Index. A tool that doesn't care what you called your files — only what's in them. Add your folders once, and everything becomes searchable: PDFs, Word docs, spreadsheets, text files, all of it. Quietly running in the background, ready whenever you need it.

I never did find that college project. But I haven't lost anything since.

A

Andre

Founder, Index

What we believe

Search should understand you

You shouldn't need to remember a filename. You should be able to search for what you know — a word, a phrase, a fragment of a sentence.

Your files stay yours

Everything runs locally. No cloud, no syncing, no servers. Index never touches your data — it just helps you find it.

Tools should be silent

Index runs quietly in the background, always up to date, never in your way. It's ready the moment you need it and invisible when you don't.
